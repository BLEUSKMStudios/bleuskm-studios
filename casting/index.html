The Final Hand
Short Film · Pre-Production · Director: Zaria Lashae

During a game of blackjack, a gambler attempts to outplay High John by mimicking his every move, only to realize too late that the game is testing something deeper than skill—and that true power cannot be learned through observation alone.

Roles Currently Casting
High JohnLead · Black American Male · 35–60
The Conqueror. High John is calm, observant, and deeply self-assured — the kind of man who carries quiet authority without ever demanding it. He is the dealer, but more than that: he is the House.
Character

He speaks little, but every gesture holds intention. His movements feel ritualistic rather than performative, suggesting a connection to something far older than the room itself. His power is almost entirely non-verbal — the performance lives in the hands, the stillness, the weight of a look.

Dialogue

Very Minimal. He is a man of few words — but when he speaks, every word lands like a full stop. His presence does the rest.

Seeking
  • Black American male, 35–60
  • Strong physical presence — commands the room through stillness, not force
  • No flashy performance — power through control and restraint
  • Comfortable with intentional, ritualistic physical movement
  • Period-appropriate aesthetic: tailored suits, hats, rings — Harlem Renaissance Dandyism-era
The PlayerLead · Caucasian Male · 25–40
The Challenger. A confident, intelligent man who believes power can be decoded through observation — and spends the film learning he was wrong.
Character

The Player carries the arc of the film — from casual confidence to quiet devastation. He studies High John closely, convinced whatever advantage the dealer holds can be replicated. His downfall is not ignorance. It is a subtle arrogance: the belief that mastery can be earned simply by watching long enough. The final moments are nearly silent: physical, internal, defeated.

Dialogue

Dialogue-heavy role. Must deliver casual, naturalistic conversation early on and shift into near-silence by the film's end. The arc of the performance is carried as much through body language as words.

Seeking
  • Caucasian male, 25–40
  • Strong dramatic range — quiet calculation early, emotional unraveling by the end
  • Lean, sharp-featured look — weathered intelligence, not polished
  • Must build tension through body language with minimal action
  • Naturalistic delivery — confidence that feels earned, not performed
The StrangerSupporting · Any Ethnicity Male · 25–40
The Messenger. Appears briefly at the start of the film — a quiet gatekeeper who sets everything in motion with a single gesture.
Character

The Stranger slides a chip across the table after the Player loses a hand. He understands the nature of the game but offers no explanation. His gesture is not generosity — it is an invitation. The test begins the moment the chip is accepted. He sets things in motion and steps back.

Dialogue

Very minimal — a few lines only. This role is almost entirely presence-based. The chip slide matters as much as anything spoken.

Seeking
  • Male, 25–40, any ethnicity
  • Sharp, composed, well-dressed
  • Must carry quiet menace and mystery without explanation
  • Comfortable with stillness and intentional, unhurried movement
Waitress / BartenderBackground · Female · 25–45
1–2 background roles set within a period backroom gambling parlor.
Dialogue

No dialogue required.

Seeking
  • Female, 25–45, any ethnicity
  • Period-appropriate look for a backroom gambling parlor
  • Ability to move naturally and confidently within a scene environment
  • Poise and presence on camera
Table PatronsBackground · Male or Female · 21+
3–5 seated background players for the gambling parlor environment.
Dialogue

No dialogue required.

Seeking
  • Male or female, 21+, any ethnicity
  • Must look natural and comfortable in a period-dressed environment
  • Ability to react naturally to surrounding scene activity
CoupleBackground · Black Male + Black Female · 21+
One couple in the background of the gambling parlor — two roles, cast together.
Dialogue

No dialogue required.

Seeking
  • One Black male and one Black female, 21+
  • Period-appropriate styling
  • Must be comfortable with intimate, affectionate on-camera blocking as a couple.

The 15th Hour
Short Film · Pre-Production · Director: Zaria Lashae

The 15th Hour is a contained psychological drama set over the course of a single retail shift. The film follows a quiet employee as they move through a routine workday alongside coworkers and customers, observing the subtle rhythms of the store as time passes. As the shift continues, small changes begin to reveal a deeper pattern about routine, presence, and what it means to be replaced.

Roles Currently Casting
DianaSupporting
A coworker who has learned how to survive within the system. Diana understands the routine, the customers, and the unspoken rules of the job. She is more emotionally adjusted than Zero and serves as a quiet guide to the environment, but there are hints of exhaustion underneath her composure. The actor should balance natural conversation with an underlying sense of fatigue and detachment.
FloydSupporting — Non-Speaking / Minimal Dialogue
A new employee who silently takes Diana's place. Floyd has little to no dialogue but must mirror the behavior and routine of the other workers with eerie familiarity. The performance should feel practiced, routine, and emotionally neutral - not robotic, just absorbed into the system. Floyd represents the cycle of replacement and the system continuing without interruption.
NPC Customer #3Day Player — 1 Scene
A regular customer. Casual, natural conversation. Represents the everyday normalcy of the store and how routine the environment is for customers compared to the workers. Performance should feel completely natural and unscripted.

Love Me Like This
Short Film · Pre-Production · Director: Zaria Lashae

Over the course of one night, a man set on making a woman fall in love with him connects with someone who isn't looking for anything, and through a series of intimate questions, they discover something real that isn't meant to last.

Roles Currently Casting
SosaMale Lead · Black American Male · 20–27
The emotional driver of the film. Sosa is intentional, grounded, and quietly persistent. He knows what he wants the moment he sees it — and he does not hide it.
Character

Sosa does not play games or hide behind detachment. He listens closely, observes deeply, and shows care through presence rather than performance. His vulnerability is direct but controlled, never forced. He is not chasing recklessly — he is choosing, and standing in that choice.

Seeking
  • Black American male, 20–27
  • Natural, grounded on-camera presence
  • Carries emotional weight without overplaying it — warmth and steadiness are key
  • Subtle confidence and emotional clarity
  • Ability to express affection through stillness, eye contact, and restraint
  • Chemistry read with Alanna required
AlannaFemale Lead · Black American Female · 20–27
The emotional center of the film. Alanna is perceptive, self-aware, and secure in her direction. She is not looking for love — nor is she avoiding it. She simply is not building her life around it.
Character

Alanna engages with Sosa openly and is intrigued by him, but remains grounded in her reality and future. She holds space without fully stepping into it — warm but measured. Present in the moment, yet never losing sight of the bigger picture.

Seeking
  • Black American female, 20–27
  • Confident, composed, and expressive without being performative
  • Conveys intelligence and emotional depth in subtle ways
  • Emotional intelligence and natural ease on camera
  • Ability to hold space without fully stepping into it
  • Chemistry read with Sosa required
